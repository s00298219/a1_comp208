My Teaching Philosophy for D&T

Approaches to teaching creativty, design and problem-solving

I believe that studying Design and Technology is essential for the development of divergent thinking in students, which powers creativity, problem-solving skills, and critical thinking; skills they will use for life. My teaching philosophy centers on nurturing students creativity, providing them with various opportunities for practical application and connecting students with real-world relevance, and inclusive and collaborative learning.

By providing a classroom environment that encourages and celebrates creativity, I believe that every student can achieve success. Through hands-on projects and open-ended design challenges, students have a chance to explore their imaginations, experiment with materials, and develop innovative solutions.

Design and Technology is not just about the theory behind design and innovation, but rather applying your knowledge to solve tangible problems. Connecting classroom learning to industry practices, students are able to become equipped with skills that are relevant.

Ultimately, my goal as a Design and Technology educator is to inspire a lifelong love for design, creativity, innovation, and problem-solving. I aim to equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to navigate a continuously changing technological industry, preparing them to become adaptable and divergent-thinking individuals. Through my teaching career, I hope to spark a passion for design and technology that will not only shape students but also encourage them to make meaningful contributions to society.
